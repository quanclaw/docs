Leagues on Billiards.com connect you with other players at a consistent skill level in your area, giving you regular competition and a structured way to measure your progress over a season. Whether you're looking for a casual weekly 8-ball night or a competitive handicap-adjusted 9-ball league, you can find and join leagues directly from the platform.

## League types available

Billiards.com hosts leagues in the most popular pool formats. League organizers choose the game type and format when creating a league, and those details are always shown on the league's listing page.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="8-ball">
    Standard 8-ball played to BCA or APA rules, depending on what the organizer specifies. The most common format on the platform. Weekly sessions typically run on a fixed night at a home venue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="9-ball">
    9-ball leagues run on the same weekly or seasonal schedule as 8-ball leagues. Some 9-ball leagues use rack-based scoring; others use a set-based format where the first player to win a set number of games takes the match.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="10-ball and straight pool">
    Less common but available in select cities. 10-ball leagues follow similar rules to 9-ball but use a full rack of ten balls. Straight pool (14.1 continuous) leagues track high run as a key performance stat.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Seasonal vs. weekly">
    **Weekly leagues** run matches every week on the same night, with a season lasting 10–16 weeks before playoffs. **Seasonal leagues** span a longer period with matches scheduled less frequently, making them easier to fit around a busier schedule.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## League formats

Each league uses one of three competitive formats. The format is set by the organizer and cannot be changed after the league starts.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Round-robin">
    Every player (or team, in team formats) plays every other player at least once during the regular season. Points are awarded for wins, and standings are sorted by total points. Tiebreakers are determined by head-to-head record and then by total games won.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Single elimination">
    A bracket-style format where each loss ends a player's season. Single-elimination leagues are common for shorter seasonal events and tournaments rather than ongoing weekly play.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Handicap-adjusted">
    Matches use a points-spotting or games-spotting system based on each player's handicap rating. A stronger player may need to win more games or more points to take the match than a weaker player does. This levels the field so players of different skill levels can compete fairly in the same league.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How handicaps work

Your handicap on Billiards.com is a numeric rating calculated from your recent match history. The platform uses a rolling window of your last 20 rated matches — weighted toward the most recent results — to produce a skill rating on a 1–10 scale for each game type you play.

When you join a handicap league, the league organizer sets the spotting table: for example, a player rated 7 playing a player rated 4 might spot the lower-rated player 2 games on the wire in a race-to-5 format. The exact spotting is determined by the difference between handicap ratings and the specific rules the organizer has configured.

<Note>
  Your handicap rating updates automatically after each rated match you log. It takes at least five logged matches in a game type before a handicap is calculated for that format.
</Note>

Handicap ratings are game-specific — your 8-ball rating and your 9-ball rating are tracked independently. Playing in both types of leagues won't cause one to affect the other.

## Joining a league

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Open leagues">
    Open leagues are visible to any logged-in user. To join, go to the **Leagues** section, search by game type, location, or venue, and click **Join league** on any open listing. Some open leagues have a maximum player count; if the league is full, you can add yourself to the waitlist.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Find a league">
        Use the Leagues search page to filter by game type, format (round-robin, handicap, etc.), distance from your location, and session day. Results show the current player count, the home venue, and the season start date.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review the rules">
        Each league listing has a **Rules** tab written by the organizer. Read it before joining — it covers match format, forfeit policy, makeup match rules, and any entry fees.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Join and pay (if applicable)">
        Click **Join league**. If the organizer has set an entry fee, you'll be prompted to pay through the platform before your spot is confirmed. Free leagues confirm immediately.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Private leagues (invitation only)">
    Private leagues don't appear in public search results. You join by accepting an invitation link sent to your registered email or by entering an invite code on the Leagues page under **Join with code**.

    <Note>
      League organizers can set up private leagues by invitation only.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Scheduling matches

Once the league season starts, each week's matchups are generated automatically based on the league format. You'll receive a notification with your scheduled opponent and a suggested play window (typically a 7-day window to complete the match).

To coordinate with your opponent:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your match card">
    Go to **My Leagues → Current matches** and click the match card for the current week.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Propose a time">
    Use the in-platform scheduling tool to propose one or more times that work for you. Your opponent receives a notification and can accept, counter-propose, or decline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the session">
    Once both players agree on a time, the match is marked as scheduled. You'll both get a reminder notification 24 hours before and again 1 hour before the match.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log the result">
    After the match, either player can log the result from the match card. Both players must confirm the score before it's counted toward standings. If there's a dispute, you can flag the result for the league organizer to review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Standings and points

League standings are updated in real time as match results are confirmed. The standings table for each league shows each player's name, number of matches played, wins, losses, and total points. Click any player's name to see their full match history for that season.

Points are awarded as follows in most round-robin formats:

- **Win**: 2 points
- **Bye or opponent forfeit**: 1 point
- **Loss**: 0 points

Organizers can customize the point values for their league, so always check the **Rules** tab for the exact system used in your league.

<Tip>
  Keep an eye on the standings page throughout the season — playoff seeding is determined by final standings, so late-season wins matter even if you're not in contention for first place.
</Tip>
