The Billiards.com community features let you build connections with other players in your area and across the country. Your profile tells other players who you are as a competitor, and the activity feed keeps you up to date on what people in your network are playing, where they're playing, and how they're doing.

## Player profiles

Every account on Billiards.com has a public profile page. Your profile is the first thing other players see when they look you up, and it reflects your identity on the platform.

A complete profile includes:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Skill level and preferred games">
    Your handicap rating for each game type you play (8-ball, 9-ball, etc.) is displayed on your profile, along with a summary label — Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, or Expert — based on your rating range. You can also list the games you prefer to play, even if you don't have enough matches logged for a handicap in those formats yet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Stats summary">
    Your profile shows a condensed stats summary: overall win percentage, total matches played, and career high run. Visitors can click through to your full stats page if you've set your stats visibility to public. You control this from your privacy settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Venue check-ins">
    Your recent venue check-ins appear in a timeline on your profile, showing where you've been playing. This helps other players find you at your regular spots and gives venues visibility from active members.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bio and social links">
    You can write a short bio (up to 200 characters) and optionally link to social media profiles. The bio appears at the top of your profile and is a good place to mention your home venue, the leagues you play in, or what you're working on in your game.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Following other players

Following a player adds their activity to your feed. You can follow anyone on Billiards.com — the follow relationship is one-directional, like social media, so you don't need their approval unless they've set their profile to private.

To follow someone:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find their profile">
    Search by username from the top search bar, or click a player's name anywhere it appears in the app — in league standings, on a venue review, or in the activity feed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Follow">
    On their profile page, click the **Follow** button. Their future activity will appear in your feed. You can unfollow at any time from the same button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse their activity">
    Once you follow someone, you can scroll their profile to see their recent match results, league activity, check-ins, and any highlights they've shared publicly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your own follower and following counts are shown on your profile. You can see the full list of who follows you and who you follow from **Settings → Account → Connections**.

## Activity feed

Your feed appears on the Billiards.com home screen when you're logged in. It aggregates activity from everyone you follow, presented in reverse chronological order.

Feed items include:

- **Match results**: A card showing who played, the final score, and the game type. If the player added a note to the match, it appears below the score.
- **League results**: Confirmed league match scores and standings updates from leagues you follow or that your connections are in.
- **Venue check-ins**: When a player checks into a venue, a brief card appears showing the venue name, location, and an optional comment.
- **Highlights**: Shared clips, run-out logs, or notable stats moments (see below).

You can like or comment on any feed item. Comments are visible to anyone who can see the original activity.

## Sharing match results and highlights

After logging a match, you have the option to share the result to your feed and to your followers. You can add a short caption — up to 280 characters — to give context, such as noting a particularly tough safety battle or a personal best high run.

To share a highlight separately from a match result:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Highlights section">
    Go to your profile and click **Add highlight**, or use **Community → Share** from the main navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the moment">
    Write a caption and optionally attach a video clip (MP4 or MOV, up to 60 seconds, 500 MB max) or a photo. Common highlights include run-out videos, break shots, and notable match moments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose who can see the highlight: **Everyone**, **Followers only**, or **Only me**. The default matches your global sharing preference, which you can set in **Settings → Privacy**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post">
    Click **Share**. The highlight appears on your profile and in your followers' feeds. You can delete it at any time from your profile's Highlights tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Messaging other players

You can send direct messages to any player who has messaging enabled on their account. Conversations are private and only visible to you and the recipient.

To start a conversation, open a player's profile and click **Message**. If they've disabled messages from non-followers, you'll need to follow each other first before the option appears.

<Info>
  Message notifications are delivered in-app and optionally by email. Adjust your notification preferences under **Settings → Notifications**.
</Info>

## Reporting and blocking

Billiards.com is built around competitive sportsmanship. If you receive a message or see content that violates community guidelines — including harassment, spam, or discriminatory language — you can report it directly from the offending item.

To report content or a user:

- On a message: tap the three-dot menu and select **Report message**.
- On a profile: scroll to the bottom and click **Report this player**.
- On a feed item or comment: click the three-dot menu and select **Report**.

Reports are reviewed by the moderation team, typically within 24 hours for direct messages and 72 hours for other content.

**Blocking** a player prevents them from viewing your profile, sending you messages, following you, or interacting with your content. To block someone, open their profile and select **Block** from the options menu. Blocking is immediate and silent — the blocked user is not notified.

<Warning>
  If you feel you are in immediate danger or are being harassed outside the platform, contact local authorities. Billiards.com moderation handles in-platform conduct only.
</Warning>

## Privacy settings

You control who can see your profile, stats, activity, and contact information from your privacy settings page.

<Note>
  Manage your profile visibility, stats sharing, and messaging preferences at any time from [Settings → Privacy](/settings/privacy).
</Note>

Key privacy options include setting your profile to public or followers-only, hiding your stats from non-followers, disabling messages from players you don't follow, and opting out of appearing in leaderboards.
