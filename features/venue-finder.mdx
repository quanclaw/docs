The venue finder helps you locate pool halls, billiards clubs, and bars with tables wherever you are. Search by your current location, a zip code, or a city name to see every listed venue in the area, complete with hours, contact info, table details, and player reviews.

## How search works

Enter a city, zip code, or street address in the search bar at the top of the Venues page, or click **Use my location** to let the platform detect your position automatically.

<Tip>
  Enable location access in your browser or app for the most accurate results.
</Tip>

Results appear as venue cards on a map and in a scrollable list. Each card shows the venue name, street address, phone number, current open/closed status, and a summary of available table types.

## What's on a venue card

Every venue listing includes the following information when the venue owner has provided it:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Contact and hours">
    The venue's phone number, website link, and full weekly hours are listed under the **Info** tab. Hours reflect the venue's local time zone. If a venue marks itself as temporarily closed, a banner appears at the top of its listing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Table types">
    Venues specify the kinds of tables on site: **bar tables** (typically 7-foot coin-op), **tournament-size tables** (9-foot), and **snooker tables** (10- or 12-foot). The table count is shown when the venue has provided it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Amenities">
    Amenity icons indicate whether a venue has a **food menu**, **on-site parking**, or a **pro shop** stocking cues, chalk, and accessories. Hover over any icon for a short description.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Filtering results

Use the filter panel on the left side of the Venues page (or the **Filters** button on mobile) to narrow your results.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Distance">
    Set a maximum radius from your search location: 5, 10, 25, or 50 miles. The default is 25 miles. All venues outside that radius are hidden from the list and the map.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Table type">
    Check one or more table types — bar tables, tournament-size, or snooker — to show only venues that have those tables. Selecting multiple types returns venues that have at least one of them.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Open now">
    Toggle **Open now** to hide venues that are currently closed based on their listed hours. This filter updates in real time as hours change throughout the day.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Rating">
    Set a minimum star rating (1–5) to filter out venues with lower community ratings. Venues with fewer than three reviews are excluded from star-rating filters to avoid skewed results.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Saving favorite venues

Once you find a venue you like, click the **Save** button (bookmark icon) on its listing to add it to your Favorites. Your saved venues appear under **My Venues → Favorites** in your account dashboard, so you can pull them up quickly without searching again.

You can also **check in** at a venue when you arrive. Check-ins appear in your activity feed and on your public profile, letting other players see where you play.

## Writing and reading reviews

Player reviews help the community find the best venues. To leave a review, open any venue listing and scroll to the **Reviews** section.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Rate the venue">
    Select 1 to 5 stars. You must have an account and be logged in to submit a rating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your review">
    Add a written review of up to 500 characters. Mention table condition, staff, atmosphere, or anything else players should know. Reviews are public and attached to your profile name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Post review**. Your review goes live immediately and can be edited or deleted from your account settings within 30 days of posting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can read existing reviews on any venue page without an account. Reviews are sorted by most recent by default, with an option to sort by highest or lowest rating.

<Warning>
  Reviews that contain personal attacks, phone numbers, or off-topic content may be removed by moderators. Repeated policy violations can result in review privileges being suspended.
</Warning>

## Claiming a venue (for owners)

If you own or manage a billiards venue listed on Billiards.com, you can claim it to update its information, respond to reviews, and add photos.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your venue">
    Search for your venue by name and address. Open the listing and click **Claim this venue** near the bottom of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify ownership">
    Enter your business email address or phone number. Billiards.com sends a verification code. You can also verify by connecting a business Google or Facebook account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete your profile">
    After verification, you can edit hours, update table counts and types, add amenity details, upload photos, and respond publicly to player reviews.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Claimed venues appear with a verified badge. Verification typically completes within a few minutes using the code method, or up to 24 hours for manual review if automated verification fails.
</Note>
