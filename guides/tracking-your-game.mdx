---
title: "Log and Track Your Billiards Matches"
description: "How to log match results, record game stats, and review your performance history on Billiards.com to measure your improvement over time."
---

Logging your matches on Billiards.com gives you a complete picture of how your game is developing. Every result you submit builds your performance history, refines your handicap rating, and surfaces patterns — like which game types you win most consistently or how your safety play has improved over a season. The more consistently you log, the more useful your stats become.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the 'Log a Match' screen">
    From your dashboard, click the **Log a Match** button in the top action bar. On mobile, tap the **+** icon at the bottom of the screen and select **Log a Match**. You can log a match at any time — during a session, right after you finish, or even the following day if you forget on the night.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the game type">
    Choose the game you played from the available formats:

    - **8-ball** — standard bar rules or BCA rules
    - **9-ball** — race to a set number of games
    - **10-ball** — called-shot variant of 9-ball
    - **Straight pool** — high-run scoring to a target score
    - **One-pocket** — each player assigned a pocket, first to 8 wins

    The game type determines which stats are available to record in the next steps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your opponent">
    Search for your opponent by their Billiards.com username. If they have an account, select them from the results so the match links to both profiles. If your opponent isn't on the platform, choose **Add as Guest** and enter their name manually — the match still counts toward your record.

    <Tip>
      Connect with your opponent's Billiards.com account so both of you get credit for the match. Linked matches are verified by both parties, which carries more weight in handicap calculations than guest matches.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the score and key stats">
    Enter the final score for the match (games won by each player). You can also record additional stats that help track your overall game quality:

    - **Innings** — total turns at the table across the match
    - **High run** — the longest consecutive run of balls you pocketed in a single inning (especially useful in straight pool)
    - **Safeties played** — how often you played a defensive shot instead of attacking

    None of these extra stats are required, but filling them in gives you richer data to review later and contributes to more accurate performance trends over time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Optionally add a note or photo">
    Use the **Notes** field to record anything worth remembering — a particularly good or bad position play, a rule dispute, or context about the conditions (tight pockets, worn cloth, etc.). You can also attach a photo, such as a shot setup you want to study later. Notes and photos are visible only to you unless you choose to share them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit — your stats update immediately">
    Tap **Submit Match**. Your profile stats update in real time: win/loss record, game type breakdown, and rolling averages all reflect the new result. If you logged the match against a verified opponent, both profiles update simultaneously.

    Your **handicap rating** also adjusts after each logged match. Wins against stronger-rated players push your rating up faster; losses to lower-rated players nudge it down. The system uses a weighted formula that smooths out outliers, so one bad night won't dramatically change your rating — consistent performance over multiple matches is what moves the needle.
  </Step>
</Steps>
