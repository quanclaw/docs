---
title: "Enter and Compete in Billiards Tournaments"
description: "How to find, register for, and compete in local and regional billiards tournaments on Billiards.com, from browsing the calendar to reporting final results."
---

Tournaments offer a different kind of competition than league play — higher stakes, faster rounds, and the chance to test your game against a wider field in a single day. Billiards.com lists local and regional events across all skill levels and game types, handles registration and bracket management in one place, and lets you follow the action in real time whether you're competing or watching from the rail.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse tournaments from the main navigation">
    Select **Tournaments** from the top navigation bar. The tournament calendar shows upcoming events as a list or calendar grid. Use the filters to narrow results by:

    - **Location** — city, state, or a set mile radius from your zip code
    - **Game type** — 8-ball, 9-ball, 10-ball, straight pool, one-pocket, or mixed
    - **Entry fee** — free events, events under a specific amount, or open range
    - **Date** — search within a specific date window or look at a rolling next-30-days view

    Tournaments you're already registered for appear highlighted at the top of the list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review tournament details">
    Click any event to open its detail page before committing. Key information to review:

    - **Format** — single-elimination, double-elimination, or round-robin. Double-elimination gives you a second chance after one loss; round-robin ensures you play a set number of matches regardless of wins and losses.
    - **Prize structure** — cash payouts, trophies, gift cards, or points toward a season ranking
    - **Table count** — more tables mean shorter wait times between rounds
    - **Rules set** — BCA, APA, or house rules, including any called-shot requirements
    - **Estimated duration** — plan your day around how long the event typically runs

    <Note>
      Some tournaments require a minimum number of logged matches or an established handicap rating to enter. These requirements are listed clearly on the detail page. If you don't yet qualify, logging more matches on your profile is the fastest way to become eligible.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Register and pay the entry fee">
    Click **Register** on the tournament detail page. If the event has an entry fee, you'll be prompted to complete payment through the platform using a saved payment method or by entering new card details. Registration is confirmed immediately after payment, and you'll receive a confirmation email with the event details.

    If registration is full, you can join the waitlist. You'll be notified and given a short window to complete payment if a spot opens before the event date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Receive your bracket assignment before the event">
    Brackets are typically published one to three days before the tournament. You'll receive a push notification or email when the bracket goes live. Open the tournament page to see your first-round opponent, table assignment (if pre-assigned), and estimated start time. Review the bracket structure so you understand how advancement works and who you'd face in later rounds if you win.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check in on the day of the tournament using the app">
    Arrive at the venue before the check-in deadline posted on the tournament page — typically 15 to 30 minutes before the first round. Open the Billiards.com app, navigate to the tournament, and tap **Check In**. This confirms your presence to the organizer and locks in your spot in the bracket. Players who miss the check-in window may be replaced by waitlisted alternates or recorded as a forfeit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Report match results as you advance through the bracket">
    After each match, one or both players submit the result through the app. Navigate to the tournament bracket, find your match, enter the score, and tap **Submit Result**. The bracket updates immediately and shows your next opponent and table assignment. If there's a dispute about a result, flag it using the **Report Issue** option on the match screen — the organizer will be notified and can adjudicate.

    <Tip>
      You don't have to be playing to follow a tournament. On any tournament page, tap **Follow** to receive live bracket updates as matches complete. It's a good way to scout competition, support friends, and stay engaged with your local scene even when you're not entered.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Final results and prize distribution">
    Once all rounds are complete, the organizer marks the tournament as finished and final standings are published on the event page. Your tournament results are added to your profile history, and any handicap or ranking implications are applied automatically.

    If the tournament includes cash or prizes, distribution is handled through the platform. You'll receive a notification with payout details and instructions for claiming your winnings. For in-person prize ceremonies, the event page will note if attendance is required to collect.
  </Step>
</Steps>
