---

Your Billiards.com profile is the foundation of your experience on the platform. It tells other players who you are, what games you play, and where you play them — and it drives the recommendations you receive for local venues, leagues, and opponents at your skill level. Keeping your profile accurate ensures you get matched with the right competition and the right opportunities.

## Navigate to profile settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the account menu">
    Select your avatar or username in the top-right corner of any page to open the account menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Profile">
    Click **Profile** in the dropdown. This opens the Profile Settings page where you can edit all of your personal details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    Update any of the fields described below, then save when you're done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editable profile fields

### Display name

Your display name appears on your profile, in league standings, on match scorecards, and in community posts. It can be your real name, a nickname, or a handle — whatever you want other players to know you by. Display names must be between 3 and 40 characters and cannot contain special characters other than hyphens and underscores.

### Profile photo

Upload a photo to help other players and venue staff recognize you.

<Info>
  Photos must be square and at least 200×200 pixels. Supported file formats are JPG, PNG, and WebP. The maximum file size is 5 MB. Your image will be cropped to a circle in most display contexts, so center your face or logo within the frame.
</Info>

### Bio

Write a short introduction — up to 160 characters — that appears just below your display name on your public profile. Use this space to share your preferred games, how long you've been playing, or what you're looking for on the platform (casual games, league competition, coaching, etc.).

### Location

Enter your city and state to help Billiards.com surface nearby venues, local leagues, and players in your area. Your location is used only for recommendations; it is not displayed publicly unless you set your profile visibility to Public.

### Skill level

Select the level that best describes your current game:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Beginner">
    You're learning the rules, basic shot selection, and cue ball control. You play recreationally and are still building consistency.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Intermediate">
    You understand position play, can run short racks, and compete regularly in casual or bar-league settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Advanced">
    You compete in organized leagues and local tournaments, have a working knowledge of all standard game formats, and run racks consistently.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pro">
    You compete at a high-level regional or national level and have verifiable tournament results.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  Changing your skill level resets your handicap calculation from the next match forward. Your historical match results are preserved, but handicap-adjusted stats will reflect the new level going forward.
</Note>

### Preferred games

Select all the game formats you play. Your selections influence league and event recommendations you receive.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Pocket billiards">
    - **8-Ball** — the most widely played format; first player to pocket their group and then the 8-ball wins
    - **9-Ball** — rotation game played with balls 1–9; fast-paced and popular in competitive play
    - **10-Ball** — similar to 9-Ball but requires called shots; considered more skill-testing at higher levels
    - **Straight Pool (14.1)** — continuous rotation game with a set point target; classic championship format
    - **One-Pocket** — each player is assigned one corner pocket; strategic and positional
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Carom billiards">
    - **Three-Cushion** — played on a pocketless table; the cue ball must contact three or more cushions before completing a carom
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Home venue

Link your regular pool hall as your home venue. This appears on your profile and helps leagues and tournaments identify their local player base. Start typing the venue name to search; select from the results to link it. You can update or remove your home venue at any time.

## Save your changes

After editing any fields, scroll to the bottom of the Profile Settings page and click **Save changes**. A confirmation message will appear at the top of the page. Changes to your display name and skill level take effect immediately across the platform.

## Linked accounts

Connect third-party accounts to make signing in faster and to verify your identity.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Google" icon="google">
    Link your Google account to sign in with one click using your Gmail credentials.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Facebook" icon="facebook">
    Connect Facebook to sign in and optionally share activity to your timeline.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Apple ID" icon="apple">
    Use Sign in with Apple for a private, secure login without sharing your email.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

To link an account, scroll to the **Linked accounts** section at the bottom of Profile Settings and click **Connect** next to the provider. You will be redirected to that provider's authorization page. To unlink an account, click **Disconnect** — note that you must have either a password or at least one other linked account before you can disconnect a provider.
