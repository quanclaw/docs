---

Billiards.com gives you granular control over what other players can see and how they can interact with you. Whether you want a fully public presence to build your reputation in the community, or a more private setup where only your league knows you're here, the privacy settings let you tune every aspect of your visibility. This page explains each option and what it does.

## Navigate to privacy settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the account menu">
    Select your avatar or username in the top-right corner of any page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Click **Settings** in the dropdown menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Privacy">
    Choose **Privacy** from the left-hand settings navigation to open the Privacy Settings page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Profile visibility

Your profile visibility setting controls who can find and view your profile page, including your display name, bio, location, home venue, and preferred games.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Public">
    Anyone on Billiards.com — including players who are not logged in — can view your profile. Your display name will appear in search results, venue player lists, and league standings visible to the public. This is the default setting and gives you the most community exposure.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Friends Only">
    Only players you follow who also follow you back (mutual follows) can view your full profile. Other logged-in users will see your display name but no additional details. You will not appear in public search results.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Private">
    Your profile is not visible to other players on the platform. The only exception is league and tournament organizers for events you are currently enrolled in — they can view your profile to verify your registration and contact details. Use this setting if you want to participate in organized play without a public presence.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Stats visibility

Your stats visibility is controlled independently from your profile visibility. This lets you, for example, keep a public profile while keeping your win/loss record and handicap private.

| Option | What others see |
|---|---|
| **Public** | Your full stats — match record, handicap, game-by-game history, and ranking — are visible to anyone who can view your profile. |
| **Friends Only** | Only mutual follows can see your stats. Other players see your profile but no performance data. |
| **Hidden** | No one outside of league and tournament organizers can see your stats. Your name may still appear in standings without associated stats. |

## Match history visibility

Control whether other players can browse your individual match logs — including opponents, scores, venues, and dates. This setting respects your stats visibility: if your stats are set to Hidden, match history is automatically hidden regardless of this setting. If you want to display your overall record but keep individual match details private, set stats to Public and match history to **Friends Only** or **Hidden**.

## Activity feed

Your activity feed shows actions like venue check-ins, match logs, league joins, and tournament registrations. You can choose which activity types appear on your profile's feed for others to see.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Venue check-ins">
    When enabled, your check-ins at pool halls are visible to others who can view your profile. This helps players in your network know where you play and when.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Match logs">
    When enabled, completed matches appear in your feed as they are recorded. This includes the game format, venue, and result, but not private scorecards unless your match history visibility is set to Public.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="League activity">
    When enabled, league joins, promotions, and division changes are shown in your feed. This is useful for players who want to share their competitive progress.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Who can message you

Control which players can send you direct messages on Billiards.com.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Everyone" icon="users">
    Any logged-in Billiards.com user can send you a message. You can still block individual users to prevent them from contacting you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="People I Follow" icon="user-check">
    Only players you actively follow can send you messages. Players you don't follow will see a message that direct messaging is restricted.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Nobody" icon="user-xmark">
    No one can send you direct messages. Existing conversations are preserved and accessible but new threads cannot be started.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Blocking players

Blocking a player prevents them from viewing your profile, messaging you, following you, or appearing in your match suggestions — regardless of your other privacy settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the player's profile">
    Navigate to the profile of the player you want to block.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the options menu">
    Select the three-dot menu (**⋯**) near the top of their profile page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Block">
    Click **Block [username]**. A confirmation dialog will appear. Confirm to apply the block immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once a player is blocked:

- They cannot view your profile, even if your visibility is set to Public.
- Any existing direct message threads between you are hidden from both sides.
- They are removed from your followers and following lists.
- You will no longer be matched against each other in league or opponent suggestions.

To review or remove blocks, go to **Account → Settings → Privacy → Blocked players**.

## Data and account

### Download your data

You can request a full export of your Billiards.com data, including your profile information, match history, stats, messages, and activity logs. Exports are generated within 48 hours and delivered as a downloadable archive to your account email address. To request an export, scroll to the **Data** section of Privacy Settings and click **Request data download**.

### Delete your account

<Warning>
  Deleting your account permanently removes all your stats, match history, and league records. This cannot be undone. Before deleting, consider downloading your data so you have a local copy of your history.
</Warning>

Account deletion removes your profile, all associated data, and cancels any active league or tournament registrations. If you are a team captain or league administrator, you must transfer those roles before your account can be deleted. To begin the deletion process, scroll to the **Account** section of Privacy Settings and click **Delete my account**, then follow the confirmation steps.
